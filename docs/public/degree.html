Degree overview

Purpose

The goal of the computer engineering degree is to produce individuals with a strong background in science and technology who can design, develop, implement, and maintain computer systems, including both hardware and software. It seeks to give students the abilities they need to take charge of projects, adjust to changes in technology, and effectively and independently solve challenging issues.

This degree adopts a comprehensive approach, encompassing every facet of computer science, from distributed systems and networks to cybersecurity and artificial intelligence. It integrates technological, scientific, ethical, and management knowledge to prepare students to face the problems of the ICT industry.

In order to ensure that graduates are prepared to keep learning and developing in a field that is changing quickly while maintaining a high employability rate, the degree ultimately aims to create information and communication technology specialists who can propel digital transformation in businesses and organizations.

Subjects

  • Fundamentals of Computer Engineering: Because this subject is so important to the degree, it has his own dedicated space.

  • Matemáticas para la ingenieria I: Is a foundational subject in the Computer Engineering degree, designed to strengthen our understanding of linear algebra and optimization. It emphasizes applying mathematical models to real-world problems, encouraging analytical and critical thinking. Through lectures, practical sessions, and collaborative work, we develop skills in solving complex engineering challenges. This subject lays the groundwork for future studies and professional applications in engineering.

  • Matemáticas Discretas: Introduces key concepts such as set functions, arithmetic, graph theory, and logic, essential for developing computational thinking. It emphasizes applying mathematical structures to areas like algorithms, databases, and data structures. Through problem-solving and formal reasoning, so we enhance our ability to abstract, analyze, and design solutions, fostering skills critical for engineering and computer science. The subject, part of the first-year curriculum, integrates lectures, practical sessions, and collaborative projects to build a strong foundation in mathematical rigor and logical reasoning

  • Introducción a la programación: Is designed to provide foundational knowledge in programming, emphasizing algorithmic thinking, coding principles, and structured problem-solving. We explore concepts such as algorithms, control structures, data types, recursion, and file operations, primarily using the C programming language. The curriculum combines theoretical instruction, practical labs, and collaborative projects to develop programming skills and encourage autonomous learning. Evaluation includes theoretical and practical exams, project work, and participation, with resources like online IDEs and virtual learning platforms supporting the learning process.

  • Administración de sistemas operativos I: Is part of the Cybersecurity and Ethical Hacking diploma, provides foundational knowledge of operating systems, focusing on their functions, architecture, and management. It includes practical training in virtualization using tools like VMware and VirtualBox, as well as the installation and configuration of Microsoft Windows for end-users and servers, including DNS and Active Directory setup. Learning is facilitated through lectures, hands-on labs, and independent work, promoting collaborative and self-directed learning.

  • Introducción a la Ciberseguridad: Provides us with a comprehensive foundation in the principles and practices of safeguarding digital systems and data. Covering topics such as cyber threats, vulnerabilities, risk assessment, and security protocols, the subject introduces key concepts like encryption, firewalls, network security, and ethical hacking. Practical activities include analyzing real-world cyberattacks, implementing basic security measures, and understanding compliance with legal and ethical standards in cybersecurity.

  • Lógica y Pensamiento crítico: This subject helps us to improve our reasoning and problem-solving skills. It focuses on understanding university values like seeking truth and combining knowledge from different areas. We learn how to think creatively and logically, write clearly, and speak confidently. This subject prepares us to analyze problems carefully and express our ideas clearly in both academic and real-world situations.

  • Gestión del conocimiento y habilidades de la persona: It aims to develop essential personal and professional skills such as self-awareness, teamwork, leadership, and effective communication. Through lectures, seminars, workshops, and mentoring sessions, we take an active role in their education, discovering and honing competencies that will help them maximize their academic experience and prepare for future professional challenges. The course covers university identity and culture, the pursuit of truth, and the development of skills and competencies like teamwork, public speaking, and time management. Evaluation includes theoretical-practical assessments, integral projects, and personal mentoring, promoting autonomous and collaborative learning, creative leadership, and a positive impact on their environment.

  • Matemáticas para la ingenieria II: It focuses on applying and developing knowledge of differential and integral calculus for real functions of one variable, and introduces differential calculus for functions of two variables. It aims to be a practical course that guides us in solving complex problems, aligning with real-world engineering tasks. The main objective is to equip us with sufficient mathematical skills to tackle common problems in computer engineering, emphasizing analytical, synthetic, reflective, and critical reasoning.

  • Electrónica y tecnología de computadores: It focuses on the fundamentals of electronics and their relationship with the physical operation of computers, peripherals, and communication systems. It covers principles of electromagnetism, semiconductors, diodes, transistors, logic families, and digital network analysis and synthesis. The main objective is to enable us to design and analyze combinational and sequential digital networks, understand simple analog circuits, and grasp the basics of semiconductors and CMOS technology. The course combines theoretical knowledge with practical laboratory sessions to provide a comprehensive understanding of both the physical and logical levels of digital systems

  • La empresa y sus procesos: It explores business structures as open social organizations and economic production units. It covers different types of companies and their economic, legal, and technological factors, with a significant focus on processes and information flows within any productive structure. The course aims to familiarize us with the role of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) in business, providing insights into technological solutions for integrated information management and external relations. The objective is to help us understand the business environment, develop viable business projects, and grasp basic concepts of accounting, finance, and data analysis, emphasizing the critical role of ICT in modern business operations.

  • Programación orientada a objetos: It introduces the object-oriented programming (OOP) paradigm, emphasizing its benefits for software quality, particularly in terms of reusability, extensibility, and reliability. The course is divided into theoretical and practical blocks, covering key OOP concepts such as classes, objects, abstraction, encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism. The primary goal is to enable us to master the analysis, design, implementation, and testing of software using OOP, with a focus on practical application through the C++ programming language. This comprehensive approach ensures that we not only understand OOP principles but also effectively apply them in real-world scenarios.

  • Historia de la ciencia y la tecnología: It offers a comprehensive journey through the history of scientific discoveries and their technological applications. It emphasizes the social context and the ideas and beliefs of the society and scientists at the time of these discoveries. The objective is to provide us with a panoramic view of significant historical events that have shaped modern history, particularly the role of scientific development. This course aims to help us understand the historical roots of their field, its connections with philosophy, mathematics, and physics, and its evolution linked to technology and system construction.

  • Administración de sistemas operativos II: It focuses on the role of operating systems in computers, emphasizing file, process, and permission management. It introduces Unix-based systems, covering installation, partitioning, shell usage, and both basic and advanced commands. The objective is to enable us to understand the basic functioning of Unix-based operating systems, starting from installation and configuration to system administration using shell commands.
